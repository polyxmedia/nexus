## Posting Strategy

**Timing:**
- LinkedIn: Tuesday-Thursday, 8-9am GMT. Finance/tech crowd scrolls before work.
- Twitter: Same days, plus evening 6-8pm GMT for US overlap.

**Sequence:**
1. Teaser first (build curiosity, get people asking "what is this")
2. War room 2-3 days later (visual hook, most shareable)
3. AI analyst + predictions 2-3 days after that (depth, credibility)

**Seeding (do this before you post):**
- Have 5-10 people ready to like and comment in the first 30 minutes. The algorithm decides in the first hour whether to push your post. Early engagement is everything.
- DM them the post link the moment it goes live. Don't just hope they see it.
- On LinkedIn, drop a comment yourself immediately after posting with a link to your site or extra context. Your first comment gets visibility.

**Engagement:**
- Reply to every comment in the first 2 hours. Every reply counts as engagement and feeds the algorithm.
- Pin the teaser video to your Twitter profile.
- If a post does well, repost 2-3 weeks later with slightly different copy. Most followers didn't see it the first time.

**Tagging for reach:**
- Tag relevant people who've posted about AI tools, quant analysis, or geopolitics. Not randomly, only when your content is genuinely relevant to their audience.
- On LinkedIn, tag no more than 3 people per post. More than that looks desperate.

**After these three land, expand with:**
- Game theory deep dive (Harsanyi/Fearon analysis is impressive to the right niche audience, save it for after you've built momentum)
- Signal convergence walkthrough
- "How I built this" technical breakdown (strong potential in dev circles)
- Live demo where the audience picks a topic and you run the analysis in real time

## Recording Notes

- Screen record at native resolution, dark theme
- Record voiceover audio separately (don't use laptop mic, even AirPods Pro are fine)
- Let each screen breathe for 2-3 seconds before talking about it
- Mouse movements should be deliberate and slow, no frantic clicking
- LinkedIn: 4:5 ratio performs best in feed (1080x1350). 16:9 is fine too.
- Twitter: 16:9 (1920x1080)
- Captions are mandatory on both platforms, most people scroll with sound off
- Keep the war room video slightly longer on the map zooms, let people absorb the visual
- For the chat demo, pick a question that sounds simple but produces a complex, impressive answer
- Show a denied prediction deliberately in Video 3, proving honesty is more compelling than cherry-picking wins
